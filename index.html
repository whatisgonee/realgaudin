Welcome to Gaudin's Corner
La Blanchisseuse, 1886, Henri de Tolouse-Lautrec.

Carmen Gaudin was a French sex worker in the 19th century. The reason why she remains a relatively famous person till this day is because of Henri de Tolouse-Lautrec – a French painter and artist, who was at the time fascinated by Gaudin. Henri had painted a dozen of paintings featuring Carmen Gaudin, many of which are now on display in the National Gallery of Art.

I have taken the surname of Carmen for no particular reason – I simply like her portraits and the way her surname sounds. In addition, it is a relatively uncommon surname, as far as I'm concerned.

In any ways, welcome to my piece of the Internet pie. This is a website where I post various things – from my unfinished projects to articles and essays on topics I find interesting. On your left side you can see a navigation bar. Click on any of the links or chat with my robotic creation below.

What is Gallery?

Gallery is a part of my website dedicated to other people's art. Usually, it's some classical stuff which I find interesting or consider those paintings to be my favourites. All art in the Gallery section of the website is in Public Domain or re-uploaded with direct permission of the artist. Paintings which you might find in the gallery is like the one above!
